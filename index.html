ATLAS is an empirical–quantitative correlation study testing which passive strategies in historical vernacular buildings remain valid under projected future climate, and which require adaptation — paired with a simulation tool that bridges description and prescription.
_workflow

The pipeline

Each case study is anchored to the nearest representative EPW / TMYx weather station. Present-day files (TMYx 2011–2025) are morphed to 2050 and 2080 horizons using CMIP6 deltas under SSP5-8.5, following the Belcher et al. (2005) morphing method with bilinear interpolation. Precipitation is intentionally not morphed.

Architectural metrics are extracted from DXF drawings: wall thermal mass as floor-plan polygons extruded to eave height (C = Σ V × VHC), window-to-wall ratio, wall thickness via the perimeter–area formula, compactness ψ (clamped at 1.0), and eave / rake depths read from measurement lines only — no geometric fallback. Each building reduces to a binary tag matrix matched against its EPW climate signature.

Correlations are inferred with logistic regression, treating country and altitude zone as confound controls because cultural and climate variables co-vary geographically. A two-zone unconditioned Grasshopper / Honeybee model (inhabited zone + attic buffer) is then simulated in EnergyPlus against present and morphed EPWs — the mechanistic step that lets correlations become defensible prescriptions.

Why simulation is mandatory

Observing that vernacular buildings correlate with their climate is descriptive. Recommending those features for new construction is prescriptive. Crossing that line on correlation alone is the naturalistic fallacy. Simulation licenses the jump — and exposes the one variable a climate analogue can never fix: solar geometry is set by latitude, not climate, so a future-Cfa Bavarian site still receives Bavarian sun angles, not the sun of a present-day Cfa site further south.

Limitations

  • Naturalistic fallacy — correlation cannot by itself justify prescription; simulation is required and remains partial.
  • Latitude-fixed solar geometry — climate-analogue matching cannot transfer solar angles; shading and gain strategies do not migrate with the climate envelope.
  • Museum relocation bias — open-air-museum buildings are assigned EPW from museum coordinates, not the original build site; orientation tags are unreliable for these unless verified against historic cadastral maps.
  • Incomplete corpus — geometry metrics exist for a subset of buildings; coordinates and EPW assignment are missing for others; Köppen classification and simulation outputs are not yet written back for most rows. live counts shown above
  • Precipitation — not morphed and unreliable for classification; future Köppen uses present-day precipitation to avoid rounding-driven misclassification.
  • Recent-warming bias in TMYx — 2011–2025 normals already embed warming, so some stations no longer classify as Dfb even where 1961–1990 normals would. Significant, not an error.
